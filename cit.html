🎨 Repainting the CIT

Using color and student input to reimagine a well-used academic space

🧩 Context

The Center for Information Technology (CIT) is one of the most frequented academic buildings at Brown, especially for CS majors. Despite its importance, the building's interior is very dreary, and often the source of student complaints.

👤 Audience

My primary audience was the students who use the CIT regularly. These students often spend long hours in the space, and their comfort and focus are directly influenced by its atmosphere. Secondary audiences included teaching staff and building administrators, who care about promoting a functional and welcoming environment.

🎯 Design Goal

To evaluate how the CIT's color palette influences student mood, focus, and comfort—and propose a student-informed, cohesive redesign.

🧠 Research & Feedback

I conducted observational walkthroughs and informal interviews. My findings included:

  • Liked: Lots of sunlights in fifth floor
  • Disliked: Dreary tones and uncoordinated furniture
  • Functional insight: Colors affect mood and movement through space

🔁 Iteration

I created multiple palettes and layout tests. The strongest concepts emphasized calming blues balanced with warm tones.

✅ Final Proposal

  • Unified wall colors per floor (cool tones for study spaces)
  • Coordinated trim and accent color scheme
  • Upgraded seating to match the palette

📚 Reflections

This project reminded me how much space affects experience. Small interventions, especially when co-designed, can create a better environment.
