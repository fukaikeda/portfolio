Designing a low-tech, high-trust workflow for handmade object storage

At Pottery @ Brown, glazed ceramics were frequently lost or mixed up. Sticky notes fell off shelves, and members didn't know where to store items. This caused stress and inefficiency in a tight space with rotating users.

üë• Audience

The storage system serves a diverse group of users at Pottery @ Brown. This include first-time ceramicists to experienced studio aides.

üéØ Design Goal

Create a clear, easy-to-follow system that lets users store, label, and find their pieces without complex software or overburdening staff.

üß† Research & Feedback

Through interviews and observations, I found:

  • Members often had trouble finding work
  • There was limited storage space
  • Studio would get messy quite fast

Based on these initial interviews and observations, I tried several ideas.

  • Sticky Notes: Failed due to reliability, would fly off
  • Etching Initials: Difficult to do on wet work, sometimes unidentifiable.
  • 'To Claim' Box:Difficult to organize

While these solutions did not completely work, they gave me valuable insights to form my final system.
